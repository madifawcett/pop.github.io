About Us

The “MAR” stands for the initials of each of our names; Coders represents a vital part of who we are, and put together, the word “Mark” appears. Endangered Animals

Although they cannot speak, animals are still valuable inhabitants of our planet. And yet, they undergo unspeakable tragedies at the hands of humans. For example, according to worldelephantday.org, “100 African elephants are killed each day.” Poachers ravage these majestic creatures for ivory and meat. Another endangered species resides in Asia. Through deforestation, panda bears lose their habitats and food supply. In fact, animalfactguide.com informs us, “there are only about 2060 pandas living in the wild.” And let’s not forget about our oceans. Sea turtles contribute positively to the health of our marine ecosystems, but according to seeturtles.org, “six of the seven sea turtle species are classified as threatened or endangered due to human actions and lifestyles. The biggest threats include…entanglement in fishing gear.” This is a direct human action which can be prevented. Humanity just needs to be aware.

Poverty Crisis

Few people would disagree with the statement that there are too many human beings living in poverty. Why, then, is it so easy to live our lives, ignoring that fact? According to globalissues.org, “almost half the world — over three billion people — live on less than $2.50 a day.” When a number like that is so big, it’s easy to be overwhelmed and lose sight of all the individuals who are swept up by the crushing tidal wave of deprivation. When basic food, water, and hygiene needs cannot be met, the effect is shattering. According to theworldcounts.com, “3.575 million people die from water-related diseases a year.” And yet, there are many organizations are dedicated to relieving these tragedies. You never know whose life you could change by making that simple choice to help.
